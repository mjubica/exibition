A Warm Welcome!

Years ago women had no right to vote for president. For many years women protested about how they did not have equal
{ = } rights as men. This kind of war went on for years. In many countries, women were not respected. After some years in the UK, this movement is best known through the work of Emmeline Pankhurst who founded the Women’s Social and Political Union (WSPU) in 1903. This group attempted to enter parliament, heckled members of parliament, damaged and chained themselves to property, and consequently faced abuse from the media and were physically assaulted by the police. This is why we want to change gender inequality with art activsm.
According to c4aa.org Artistic Activism is a dynamic practice combining the creative power of the arts to move us emotionally with the strategic planning of activism necessary to bring about social change. There are many people doing art activism for many reasons but some people that are doing Art Activism for Gender Equality are Guerrilla Girls, Claudia Mesch, Judy Chicago and many more.
