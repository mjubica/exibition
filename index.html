A Warm Welcome!

We are 5th-grade students at A.C.T and as part of our exhibition, we are looking at our passions and the SDGs. We want to make a change by connecting our passions to the SDG goal we choose. Our passion is Visual Art but we have different types of Visual Art Marins are lego building Arber’s is photography Arons is a digital animation and Seidy’s is industrial designing. We are gonna use this passion of ours to help change the world. Gender Equality is a very big issue in some countries and it's an issue in Albania too this is why we choose this SDG. We are linking our passion for Visual ARt to Gender equality by doing Art Activism. Art Activism has many different types of VIsual Art, so this is how we are linking our passion to SDG’s Gender Equality.
Years ago women had no right to vote for president. For many years women protested about how they did not have equal { = } rights as men. This kind of war went on for years. In many countries, women were not respected. After some years in the UK, this movement is best known through the work of Emmeline Pankhurst who founded the Women’s Social and Political Union (WSPU) in 1903. This group attempted to enter parliament, heckled members of parliament, damaged and chained themselves to property, and consequently faced abuse from the media and were physically assaulted by the police.
Our third and final line of inquiry is “How does Art activism help promote Gender Equality”. The first sub-question is “What is Art Activism?”. According to c4aa.org Artistic Activism is a dynamic practice combining the creative power of the arts to move us emotionally with the strategic planning of activism necessary to bring about social change. My second sub-question is “Who is already doing Art Activism?”. There are many people doing art activism for many reasons but some people that are doing Art Activism for Gender Equality are Guerrilla Girls, Claudia Mesch, Judy Chicago and many more.
